👥 Guest Management Ke 6 Golden Rules

🔢
Guest count = sabse important budget decision — 1 extra guest = ₹2,000-4,000 extra (catering + invite + gift). 50 extra guests = ₹1-2 lakh extra. Pehle number fix karo, phir baaki sab plan karo.
📱
WhatsApp group RSVP system banao — "Wedding Planning — Priya & Rahul" group banao, sab invites isme. RSVP form link share karo ya directly message karo. 3 reminders: 6 weeks, 3 weeks, 1 week before deadline.
🪑
Seating chart din pe change mat karo — 1 week pehle finalize karo aur print karwa lo. Venue entrance pe display board lagao. Last-minute changes confusion create karte hain. Buffer table (1-2 tables empty) zaroor rakhein.
🧳
Outstation guests = extra care — Welcome kit mein: itinerary, hotel + venue address, emergency numbers, local transport options, weather info. Welcome dinner eve of wedding — outstation guests ko appreciated feel karaata hai.
👑
VIP seating = strategic — Stage ke 2-3 rows mein: parents, grandparents, close family. Elderly guests ko aisle ke paas rakhein (easy movement). Close friends stage ke baju — unki reactions photos mein best aate hain.
📊
10-15% no-show expect karo — Catering ko 90% ka count do, 100% ka nahi. Invitees ka 10-15% last minute cancel karte hain. Buffer se food waste bhi kam hoga aur extra cost bhi nahi.

Aksar Pooche Jane Wale Sawaal

Size categories: Intimate (50-100), Medium (100-200), Large (200-400), Grand (400+). Budget formula: Total guest budget = Guests × ₹2,500 (avg per person all-inclusive). 200 guests = minimum ₹5 lakh guest cost alone. Venue capacity bhi consider karo — overcrowding se sabka experience kharab hota hai.
Strategy: (1) Conflict wale groups ko physically opposite sides pe rakhein — left vs right, (2) Ek trustworthy family member ko "buffer coordinator" banao, (3) VIP uncomfortable hoge aise situation mein main stage pe lao briefly, (4) Meals timing same rakho — same time pe sab bhojan karein = interaction forced nahi hoga, (5) Photos ke liye groups alag time pe bulao.
Best system: (1) Google Sheets shared spreadsheet — Name, Phone, Attending (Y/N), Guests Count, Dietary, Notes. (2) WhatsApp replies screenshot karo + update karo. (3) 3 reminder waves: 6 weeks before (first ask), 3 weeks (reminder), 1 week (final deadline). (4) Expected no-show = 12-15% for Indian weddings. Final caterer count = confirmed count × 0.95.
Must-do: (1) Hotel block booking 2-3 months pehle — negotiate group rates, (2) Welcome kit: printed itinerary, local transport, emergency numbers, (3) Group transport for main ceremonies, (4) Welcome dinner eve of wedding, (5) Day-of coordinator specifically for outstation guests. Budget: ₹500-1000 per outstation guest for coordination costs. Worth it — impressed outstation guests = lifelong good memories.
Kids-specific planning: (1) Kiddy corner/play area — bouncy castle ya supervised play zone, (2) Kids menu alag — simple familiar food, (3) Kids table alag — parents nearby but kids saath, (4) Seating: aisle ke end mein — easy exit for toilet/crying, (5) Event timing — main ceremonies kids ke nap time se bachao, (6) Baby care room if 10+ infants. Ages 8-12 ke liye designated helper assign karo.
Protocol: (1) Hard cutoff date set karo — "2 weeks pehle ke baad koi changes nahi", (2) Buffer seats: Seating chart mein 2-3 "flex seats" rakhein, (3) Buffer food: Caterer ko 5% extra order, (4) Last minute additions: already printed chart mein "Table 15 — Open" row rakhein, (5) Removals: caterer ko 1 week pehle final count, decorator ko 3 days pehle. Venue ko at least 5 days pehle inform karo.
